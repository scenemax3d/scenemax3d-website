A predefined shortcut expression gives a name to a condition.
Use it when the same condition is needed in more than one place.
In this example, @allow_attack means the player is not colliding, not jumping, and not knocked out.
The if line can then read like plain gameplay logic: if @allow_attack.
Shortcut expressions keep complex conditions readable and reduce mistakes when the same rule is reused.
