```wait 10 seconds```
wait pauses the current command flow for a specific amount of time. In this example the program will stop for 10 seconds before continue running.
The commands after wait do not run until the wait time is finished.
```
header.print "Door opens in 2 seconds"
wait 2 seconds
header.print "Door opened"
```
In this example, the warning appears first, then SceneMax waits 2 seconds, then the opened message appears.
Use wait for timing: delayed doors, delayed explosions, countdowns, stopping sounds, or letting an animation finish.
Wait is simple, but it is important because it controls the order and timing of events. It is important to mention that **wait** command doesn't stop the game loop - it doesn't freeze the game.
