Motion Easing functions explains how motion becomes smoother and more intentional.
Use this when straight constant-speed motion feels too robotic. Easing gives motion a beginning, middle, and ending feel.
In a fighting game, this is what makes a punch, jump, dodge, replay path, or knockback feel responsive instead of random.
[wait: 2]
```
player => sinbad
when key space is pressed once do 
  player.move left 4 in 1.5 seconds [color:00FF00]ease in[color:default]
  player.move right 4 in 1.5 seconds [color:00FF00]ease out[color:default]
end do
```
In this sample, Sinbad movement feels realistic due to the ease-in movement to the left followed by ease-out movement to the right. It is possible to combine easing in and out in the same movement.
```
player.rotate(y 90) in 0.6 seconds ease in out
```
affecting both start and end.
