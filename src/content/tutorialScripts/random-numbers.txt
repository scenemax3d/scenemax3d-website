rnd creates a random number from 0 up to one less than the number you give it.
rnd(4) can return 0, 1, 2, or 3.
This works very well with arrays because array indexes also start at 0.
In this example, the random number chooses one reward from the rewards array.
Use random numbers for enemies, sounds, rewards, spawn positions, animations, and replay variety.
