An argument is a value passed into a procedure.
Use arguments when the same behavior should work on different objects or values.
In this example, spin_model accepts one argument named m.
The procedure can spin Sinbad, then spin Ninja, without needing two separate procedure definitions.
Arguments make procedures flexible and prevent repeated copies of almost identical code.
