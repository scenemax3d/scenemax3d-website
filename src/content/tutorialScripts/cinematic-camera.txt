A game cinematic camera is a camera system used to create movie-like shots inside a game.
[wait: 1]
Instead of simply following the player, it controls the camera for dramatic effect: angles, movement, zoom, focus, timing, transitions, and framing.
[wait: 1]
The cinematic camera consists of one or more oval rigs allowing the camera to move along several parts of the rigs in a certain speed while focusing on a specific target.
[wait: 1]
The camera rigs are easily set in design-time and easily activated in run-time.
