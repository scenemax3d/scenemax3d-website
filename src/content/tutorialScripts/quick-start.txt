Your SceneMax3D Studio comes with a simple **"Hello World"** sample program. When you open SceneMax3D for the first time, you will see a folder named **"Hello World"** containing a single script called **"main"**.

To open and edit the contents of the **"main"** script, simply double-click it.

On the right side, in the editor panel, you will see the script's code. Let's take a closer look:

```sys.print "Hello World!"```

The first line displays the text **"Hello World!"** on the screen.

```skybox.show solar system```

The second line displays a live **Solar System** skybox in the scene.

```d => dragon; d.fly loop```

The last line consists of two commands. The first creates a dragon and places it in the scene. The second starts the dragon's flying animation and plays it continuously in a loop.

Notice that the dragon object is named **"d"**. This name is used later in the script whenever we want to refer to that specific dragon.

The symbol **"=>"** means **"is a"**. Therefore, in our sample code, **"d => dragon"** means that **d is a dragon**.

[img: /assets/tutorials/getting-started/pasted-2026-06-09T19-45-46-827Z.png]

Beginner programmers can also use the words **"is a"** instead of the **"=>"** symbol, and the result will be exactly the same.

To run the program, click the **Play** button located on the vertical toolbar in the upper-left corner of the Studio.

If the program runs successfully and you see a flying dragon on the screen, then SceneMax3D has been installed correctly and is ready to use.
